About Me

My name is Servaas and I am currently pursuing a double degree in Aerospace Engineering and Applied Mathematics with a focus on Computational Science and Engineering at Delft University of Technology. I have a strong background in Aerospace Engineering, with a Bachelor's degree in the same field and a minor in Computational Science and Engineering.

In addition to my studies, I am currently working as a Simulation Engineer at Bladesense, where I am developing digital twin simulations for aircraft inspection automation. In my spare time, I am involved in several extracurricular activities related to my fields of study. For example, I am currently working with NLR and the TU Delft Robotics Institute on the continuation of my graduation project a "Swarm of Aircraft Inspection Drones".

If you would like to see some of my work, please check out my portfolio. There, you can find examples of projects I have completed, such as a custom motion capture system for indoor positioning and a numerical model of fishing nets using OpenFOAM. These projects demonstrate my skills and experience in Aerospace Engineering, Applied Mathematics, and Computational Science and Engineering.

Projects

Swarm of Aircraft Inspection Drones

I developed a simulation of an aircraft inspection performed by a swarm of drones. This simulation included full 6DOF drone kinematics, outer loop drone control, drone camera simulations, pathfinding, and collision avoidance. The project was awarded the 2500 euro NLF Aerospace WO Technical Award.

View Project

Numerical Modelling of Fishing Nets

I worked on a team to develop a numerical model of fishing nets using a two-way non-linear cloth simulation. My specific responsibility was to implement the fluid simulation in OpenFOAM. We developed a highly efficient simulation that accurately modeled the behavior of fishing nets in 3D and were able to generalize an approach in the literature to work for arbitrary net shapes. Through this project, I gained valuable experience with OpenFOAM.
